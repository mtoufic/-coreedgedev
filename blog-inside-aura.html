Inside AURA: twenty villas, four levels, one standard

AURA is intentionally rare — a low-density community of just twenty detached villas. Once they are gone, they are gone. Here is what is actually behind the name.

It is easy to call a project exclusive. AURA earns the word the only way that counts: by the number itself. Twenty villas. One gated community. No towers, no phases stacked behind the launch — a single, finite collection on Sheikh Zayed's Green Belt.

Built around privacy, not just space

A low-density layout only matters if the design protects it. AURA is planned around a tree-lined boulevard spine with landscaped walkways, a single controlled gated entry, and dedicated kids' play zones inside the compound. And every villa has its own private pool — privacy that doesn't depend on a shared facility.

An architecture meant to endure

The material language is deliberate and consistent: travertine, glass, presence. Every facade, setback, and finish speaks the same vocabulary. AURA isn't built to impress on day one — it's built to hold its character over the years it will be lived in.

Positioned where Giza connects

The Green Belt address keeps AURA close to the arteries that matter — roughly 20 minutes to Sphinx International Airport, 25 to Mall of Arabia, 20 to the Cairo–Alex Desert Road, and just 2 minutes to Mountain View Chillout Park. Connected, without sacrificing the quiet that makes it worth coming home to.
